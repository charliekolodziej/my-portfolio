Charlie Kolodziej is a writer, editor and big talker living in Chicago.

Kolodziej’s work has appeared in the New York Times, the Chicago Tribune, the Chicago Reader, Block Club Chicago, and the South Side Weekly (among other pubs). They are the cofounder and managing editor of Write That Down!, a quarterly magazine capturing Chicago’s essence by collecting art and artifacts from people who call the city home.


Kolodziej’s journalistic passion lies in telling stories that challenge binary perspectives on people and politics. Their undergraduate research on the rise of LGBTQ+ gun owners, was published in the Spring 2023 issue of the Chicago Journal of Sociology and received an honorable mention at the University of Chicago’s thesis awards. You can read the full paper here.


Kolodziej spent the last two years helping support individuals negatively impacted by Illinois’ criminal legal system through their work at Cabrini Green Legal Aid. They are currently a masters candidate at Northwestern's Medill School of Journalism.


They also play the drums—ba-dum tsh.
